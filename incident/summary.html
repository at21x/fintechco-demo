Incident PAY-4471

Payment processing briefly rejected valid Yen & Dinar payments

Resolved Severity: SEV-2

For about 24 minutes on the night of July 15–16, 2026, a software bug caused the payment system to miscalculate amounts for two currencies — Japanese Yen and Bahraini Dinar. Legitimate Yen payments were wrongly flagged as fraud, and Dinar payments failed to reconcile. At the worst point, roughly 1 in 3 payments in the affected window failed. No customer funds were lost — every affected payment was held or rejected, never mis-settled. On-call engineers were paged automatically, found the cause, and restored normal processing within 14 minutes; a permanent fix shipped the next day.

Resolution

  • Mitigated same night — error rate back under 5% within 14 minutes of paging, and fully back to baseline (~1%) by 00:11 UTC.
  • Permanent fix shipped the next day — the root cause was corrected and new automated tests were added to prevent recurrence.
  • No funds lost — every affected payment was held or rejected, never mis-settled.
  • Follow-up work in progress — resuming normal automated processing for the two affected currencies, and auditing other code for the same class of bug.
